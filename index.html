About Me

I am Chenyang Yuan, a third-year PhD student in Biostatistics at Emory University. I am jointly advised by Dr. Jian Hu and Dr. Zhaohui Qin, working on projects related to spatial transcriptomics. I have also worked with various collaborators including Dr. Yong Teng, Dr. Yi-An Ko, and Kirk Easley. Before joining Emory, I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen, where I initiated my research under the guidance of Dr. Tianwei Yu and Dr. Jicong Fan.

Research Interests

My research interests center around statistical genomics and computational biology, with a particular focus on machine learning and its applications in spatial and single-cell genomics data. My methodological and collaborative research topics include:

  • spatially resolved transcriptomics
  • density-based clustering
  • spatial point pattern analysis
  • Alzheimer's disease
