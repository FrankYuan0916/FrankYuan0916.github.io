About Me

I am Chenyang Yuan, a first year PhD student in Biostatistics at Emory University. I am advised by Dr. Jian Hu at the Department of Human Genetics, working on projects related to spatial transcriptomics. I am also serving as a research assistant at Dr. Yong Teng's lab at Winship Cancer Institute. Meanwhile, I collaborate with Dr. Yi-An Ko on cardiovascular diseases. Before joining Emory, I received my Bachelor's degree in Statistics from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK-Shenzhen) in 2022. I was advised by Prof. Tianwei Yu and Prof. Jicong Fan, working on a project on single-cell clustering.

Research Interests

I am interested in research topics related to statistical genetics and genomics. In particular, I am interested in machine learning and its applications on spatial transcriptomics and single-cell data. My methodological and collaborative research topics include:

  • spatial transcriptomics
  • spatial point pattern analysis
  • single-cell RNA-sequencing
  • cancer pathology and immunology
  • cardiovascular diseases
